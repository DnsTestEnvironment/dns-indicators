--- title: German Sustainable Development Strategy permalink: /en/publications_strategy/ language: en layout: page ---

From the fight against hunger and poverty to affordable and clean energy to climate protection or the goal of livable, sustainable cities: As part of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, the 193 member states of the United Nations have defined 17 global goals for sustainable development.

Since 2016, the German sustainability strategy has also been based on these 17 global goals. On March 10, 2021, the German Federal Cabinet agreed on the further development of the German Sustainability Strategy With the decision of the Federal Cabinet of 30 March 2022 (Policy Decision) , the Federal Government confirmed the strategy.

Under the leadership of the Federal Chancellery, all ministries are involved in implementing the German Sustainability Strategy, which is further developed on a regular basis. The next further development is scheduled for the end of 2024.

The central steering committee for the strategy is the State Secretaries' Committee for Sustainable Development, chaired by Sarah Ryglewski, Minister of State to the Federal Chancellor, to whom the Federal Cabinet transferred responsibility for sustainable development in the Federal Chancellery in August 2022.
