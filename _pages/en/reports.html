New Edition of the German Sustainable Development Strategy

Extract from the publication „WISTA - Wirtschaft und Statistik“, 1/2017

On January 11, 2017, the German Federal Cabinet approved the new edition of the German Sustainable Development Strategy. This revision represents the most comprehensive further development of the strategy since its conception in 2002 and is in line with the United Nations' 2030 Agenda. In this context, the new edition is the essential framework for the national implementation of the 2030 Agenda and its 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). The Federal Statistical Office has been commissioned by the German government to analyze and monitor the Sustainable Development Strategy since 2006. It produces the indicators that measure the achievement of the goals in the individual thematic areas and publishes so-called indicator reports every two years.

Machbarkeitsstudie zur Darstellung der Indikatoren der Deutschen Nachhaltigkeitsstrategie nach Bundesländern

Feasibility study on the presentation of the indicators of the German Sustainable Development Strategy by federal states (only available in German) In addition to the federal government, the majority of the German states have their own Sustainable Development Strategies. Some state governments have already aligned these with the 17 global sustainable development goals of the 2030 Agenda; in other states, there are efforts to adapt or develop a state-specific Sustainable Development Strategy.

The heads of the state and senate chancelleries of the Federation and the Länder (CdS) have decided to develop uniform indicators and evaluation standards for the Federal Government and the Länder for monitoring the success of the DNS. The Federal Statistical Office was asked by the Bund Länder Erfahrungsaustausch Nachhaltigkeit (BLE NH) - a body of the federal and state departments responsible for sustainability - to provide an overview for presenting the indicators of the DNS by federal state. The feasibility study presented here corresponds to the advanced overview.

Press releases

Federal Government achieved four of its twelve sustainability goals for 2020

Press release No. 121 of 10 March 2021

WIESBADEN – Today, 10 March 2021, the Federal Government has adopted the German Sustainable Development Strategy (DNS) 2021. To coincide with this, the Federal Statistical Office (Destatis) has checked to what extent the Federal Government achieved its goals for 2020. In the 72 DNS target areas, twelve goals were to be concretely achieved by 2020. The results regarding the goals of the German Sustainable Development Strategy are available in the new indicator report “Sustainable Development in Germany” and on the DNS online platform.

The sustainability dialogue of the Federal Government starts: sustainability indicators available interactively

Press release No. 388 of 1 October 2020

WIESBADEN – Today, 1 October 2020, the Federal Government has started the public dialogue on the further development of the German Sustainable Development Strategy. This was preceded by a first participative dialogue process from October 2019 to February 2020, including several regional conferences. At the conferences, the Federal Government discussed the update of the Sustainable Development Strategy with representatives from the economy, science and the civil society. The dialogue version of the Sustainable Development Strategy published today is the outcome of the dialogue series. At the same time, the Federal Statistical Office (Destatis) has provided the Sustainable Development Strategy indicators on a special page of its online platform on sustainable development in Germany in an interactive and map-based form.

German Sustainable Development Strategy: monitoring now in an interactive format

Press release No. 053 of 19 February 2020

WIESBADEN – The Federal Statistical Office (Destatis) now provides data for the indicators of the German Sustainable Development Strategy in an interactive and map-based format on its Online platform on sustainable development in Germany. The new format of data provision goes online on the occasion of the Dialogue Conference on the German Sustainable Development Strategy held in Bonn on 19 February 2020. The data allow monitoring the achievement of the Federal Government’s more than 60 goals of the Sustainable Development Strategy. These include, for instance, reduced greenhouse gas emissions, better education and working conditions, more affordable housing, lower government debt levels, gender equality, species diversity and better trade chances for developing countries.
